About Me

As you can imagine, most of my scientific interests are in Informatics. Specifically, I like programming microcontrollers, such as Arduino. I also love video games. Some of my favorite video games are Grand Theft Auto and Forza Horizon. In my free time, I like to study the Unity game engine and my goal is to create something like a clone game that combines GTA and Forza Horizon games, just for fun and experimentation NOT for commercial purposes, putting my personal details on this project. The purpose of this portfolio is to demonstrate some of the work that I have done to achieve this and also my skills in programming. As you can see in the following videos and photos, I have created a virtual representation of myself (avatar) as the main character of the game. The project, of course, is not completed, it requires much time and I work on it only when I have free time. I use two programs for its development, Cinema 4D for 3d modeling and Unity game engine for the building. Many times I use animations from Mixamo or in rare cases I can do motion capture with Kinect V1, using MoCap asset in Unity. I also modify ready 3d models from Sketchfab and Gtainside. I take 3d models from those sites and after I modify them into Cinema4d, to fit them to the requirements of the game, I import them into Unity in fbx format and I use some of the Unity assets, such as Realistic Car Controller (to simulate the driving physics of a vehicle) for example, to give them life. In the following examples, you will see a list of some very specific assets, that I am using. I intend to use some of this little experience and knowledge for developing my master's thesis in "Informatics Engineering".

Driving Car

In this video I'm driving my favorite car (BMW 850CSI). The car model is from Gtainside (website, which contains game mods for all games in the Grand Theft Auto series). I converted it in fbx format and modified it, using Cinema4d. The driving physics are from the Realistic Car Controller asset, which I also modified, so that the car has working dashboard and also better gear changes and camera behavior. I've also added a script for mesh deformation, when the car hits an object. I also used render texture for interior mirrors and raycasts for the opening parts (as seen, when it is close to the wall, the door does not open). Driver animations are done with FinalIK asset, which uses Inverse Kinematics to adjust the position and rotation of the driver's bodyparts, to fit.

Character Controller

As you can see in this video, the player character is my self. Specifically I created my avatar (only the head), using a photo of mine. I uploaded that photo to Avatar SDK webpage, which creates 3d avatar models from selfies. After that, I found the body in Skecthfab, and I connected the body with the head in Cinema 4d. After I created the virtual version of my self, I used Adobe Mixamo website, which can rig a humanoid character automatically. Then I used Third Person Motion Controller, to give to my character life. I also used some other assets like LipSync, MecFootPlacer, ThirdPersonCamera, etc., to setup other parameters, like the proper feet placement on the ground, the camera's colliders, the motion of eyes and the blink of eyelashes.
